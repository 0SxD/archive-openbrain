### Octopus Neuroscience and Distributed Cognition
1. **"The Mind of an Octopus"** — Science Friday / various university lectures — Search: `octopus intelligence neuroscience mind`
   - Why: Octopus nervous system overview — 2/3 of neurons in the arms, ganglion autonomy, chemotactile processing. Core to L1 (Active Sensing) and L2 (Ganglion).

2. **"Peter Godfrey-Smith: Other Minds — The Octopus and the Evolution of Intelligent Life"** — Talks at Google / RSA / various — Search: `Peter Godfrey-Smith Other Minds talk`
   - Why: Godfrey-Smith is THE philosopher-biologist for cephalopod cognition. His framework of octopus consciousness as distributed selfhood is foundational to OpenBrainLM's architecture.

3. **"Octopus RNA Editing — How Cephalopods Rewrite Their Own Brains"** — PBS Eons / SciShow / various — Search: `octopus RNA editing cephalopod brain`
   - Why: Cephalopods edit their own RNA at rates 10-100x higher than mammals. Direct biological basis for L8 morphogen agent (self-modification layer).

### Insect Colony Intelligence and Swarm Behavior
4. **"Deborah Gordon: The Emergent Genius of Ant Colonies"** — TED — Search: `Deborah Gordon TED ant colonies`
   - Why: Gordon's work on harvester ants demonstrates colony-level computation without central control. Foundational for L3 (Stigmergy + Swarm).

5. **"Thomas Seeley: Honeybee Democracy"** — Various university talks — Search: `Thomas Seeley honeybee democracy decision making`
   - Why: Seeley's work on quorum sensing and swarm decision-making in honeybees. Direct basis for L6 (Relevance Detection — quorum sensing component).

6. **"Stigmergy: How Insects Build Without a Blueprint"** — Santa Fe Institute / complexity channels — Search: `stigmergy insect colony intelligence emergence`
   - Why: Stigmergy (indirect coordination through environmental modification) is the core mechanism in L3. Termite mound construction, ant trail pheromones.

### Human Brain Architecture
7. **"The Basal Ganglia — Action Selection and Inhibition"** — Neuroscientifically Challenged / various neuro channels — Search: `basal ganglia action selection inhibition neuroscience`
   - Why: Inhibition-by-default is OpenBrainLM's core architectural principle. The basal ganglia suppress ALL actions; specific actions are RELEASED via disinhibition. Direct basis for L4.

8. **"The Hippocampus — Memory, Place Cells, and Spatial Navigation"** — MIT OpenCourseWare / Neuroscience lectures — Search: `hippocampus memory formation place cells neuroscience lecture`
   - Why: Hippocampus = L5 memory layer. Spatial mapping, episodic memory formation, memory consolidation during sleep. Core to the hippocampus agent.

9. **"The Default Mode Network — The Brain at Rest"** — Various neuroscience channels — Search: `default mode network brain resting state neuroscience`
   - Why: The DMN activates during internal mentation — mind-wandering, self-reflection, creative thinking. Direct biological basis for L8 (Pathos layer).

10. **"The Amygdala — Emotion, Fear, and Relevance Detection"** — Neuroscience lectures / Khan Academy — Search: `amygdala relevance detection emotion fear neuroscience`
    - Why: The amygdala does NOT just process fear — it detects SALIENCE. This reframing is critical. Basis for L6 relevance detection alongside quorum sensing.

## 2. arXiv Papers (free PDFs — distill into LM-LTM repo)

### Octopus and Cephalopod Neuroscience
1. **"Behavioral and neuronal bases of safety in numbers in zebrafish (and cephalopod nervous system reviews)"**
   - Search: `arxiv cephalopod nervous system distributed processing review`
   - Why: Academic reviews of cephalopod neural architecture — 350M+ neurons, 2/3 in arms, ganglion independence.

2. **"Trade-off between transcriptome plasticity and genome evolution in cephalopods"**
   - Authors: Liscovitch-Brauer et al. (2017)
   - Published in Cell (not arXiv — search Cell journal), but widely discussed
   - Search: `cephalopod RNA editing transcriptome plasticity genome evolution`
   - Why: The landmark paper showing cephalopods sacrifice genome evolution for RNA recoding flexibility. 60% of RNA transcripts edited in squid/octopus brain.

### Computational Neuroscience — SPAUN and NEF
3. **"A Large-Scale Model of the Functioning Brain"**
   - Authors: Eliasmith et al. (2012)
   - Published in Science (DOI: 10.1126/science.1225266)
   - Why: The original SPAUN paper. 2.5M neurons, performs 8 cognitive tasks. THE reference architecture for OpenBrainLM's computational model.

4. **"Neural Engineering: Computation, Representation, and Dynamics in Neurobiological Systems"**
   - Author: Chris Eliasmith, Charles H. Anderson
   - Note: This is a book (MIT Press, 2003), but NEF papers are available
   - Search: `arxiv neural engineering framework Eliasmith computation representation dynamics`
   - Why: The Neural Engineering Framework — how to encode, transform, and decode information in neural populations. Mathematical foundation for SPAUN.

5. **"Spaun 2.0: Extending the World's Largest Functional Brain Model"**
   - Authors: Choo, Eliasmith (various extensions)
   - Search: `arxiv SPAUN 2.0 large-scale brain model Eliasmith`
   - Why: Extensions to the original SPAUN — improved motor control, adaptive learning, working memory.

### Free Energy Principle and Active Inference
6. **"The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory?"**
   - Author: Karl Friston (2010)
   - Published in Nature Reviews Neuroscience (DOI: 10.1038/nrn2787)
   - Why: The canonical FEP paper. "Biological agents minimize free energy (prediction error) to maintain homeostasis." This is the verifier agent's theoretical foundation.

7. **"Active Inference: A Process Theory"**
   - Authors: Karl Friston, Thomas FitzGerald, et al. (2017)
   - Published in Neural Computation
   - Search: `arxiv active inference process theory Friston 2017`
   - Why: Extends FEP from perception to action — agents don't just predict, they ACT to confirm predictions.

8. **"A Step-by-Step Tutorial on Active Inference and its Application to Empirical Data"**
   - Authors: Ryan Smith, Karl Friston, Christopher Whyte (2022)
   - arXiv: 2201.03904
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.03904
   - Why: Practical tutorial for implementing active inference. Bridges theory to code.

### Consciousness Theories
9. **"Integrated Information Theory (IIT) 4.0"**
   - Authors: Tononi et al. (2023)
   - Search: `arxiv integrated information theory 4.0 Tononi 2023`
   - Why: The latest version of IIT. Defines consciousness as integrated information (phi) with specific mathematical axioms and postulates.

10. **"Global Workspace Theory (GWT) and Its Variants"**
    - Authors: Baars, Franklin, et al.
    - Search: `arxiv global workspace theory consciousness Baars computational model`
    - Why: GWT models consciousness as a broadcast mechanism — information in the "workspace" becomes globally available. Maps to OpenBrainLM's orchestrator ignition.

11. **"Attention Schema Theory: A Mechanistic Account of Subjective Awareness"**
    - Author: Michael Graziano
    - Search: `arxiv attention schema theory Graziano consciousness`
    - Why: AST proposes that the brain models its own attention — a "schema" for what attention is doing. Relevant to the prefrontal (metacognition) agent.

### Hebbian Learning and Synaptic Plasticity
12. **"Spike-Timing-Dependent Plasticity: A Comprehensive Overview"**
    - Search: `arxiv spike timing dependent plasticity STDP review comprehensive`
    - Why: STDP is the biological mechanism for "neurons that fire together wire together." OpenBrainLM's Hebbian plasticity cross-cutting layer implements this principle.

13. **"Synaptic Plasticity and Memory: An Evaluation of the Hypothesis"**
    - Authors: Martin, Grimwood, Morris (2000)
    - Published in Annual Review of Neuroscience
    - Why: The canonical review linking synaptic plasticity (LTP/LTD) to memory formation. Foundation for L5 memory consolidation.

### Swarm Intelligence and Stigmergy
14. **"Swarm Intelligence: From Natural to Artificial Systems"**
    - Authors: Bonabeau, Dorigo, Theraulaz
    - Note: This is a book (Oxford, 1999), but related papers are available
    - Search: `arxiv swarm intelligence stigmergy self-organization review`
    - Why: The canonical reference on swarm intelligence algorithms derived from insect colonies. Ant Colony Optimization, particle swarm optimization.

15. **"Self-Organization in Biological Systems"**
    - Authors: Camazine, Deneubourg, Franks, et al.
    - Note: Book (Princeton, 2001), but derivative papers available
    - Search: `arxiv self-organization biological systems emergence collective behavior`
    - Why: Mathematical treatment of how order emerges from local interactions without central control. Core principle behind L3.

16. **"Collective decision-making in honeybee swarms"**
    - Authors: Seeley, Visscher, et al.
    - Search: `arxiv collective decision making honeybee swarm quorum sensing Seeley`
    - Why: Mathematical modeling of how bee swarms make collective decisions via quorum thresholds. Direct model for L6.

## 3. GitHub Repositories (free, open source)

### Computational Neuroscience Frameworks
1. **nengo/nengo**
   - Stars: ~800+
   - License: Various (Free for non-commercial)
   - URL: https://github.com/nengo/nengo
   - Nengo is Eliasmith's Neural Engineering Framework in code. Build brain models with spiking or rate neurons. SPAUN was built on this. The most directly relevant repo for OpenBrainLM.

2. **nengo/nengo-gui**
   - License: Various
   - URL: https://github.com/nengo/nengo-gui
   - Interactive visualization for Nengo brain models. See neural populations fire in real time. Useful for understanding SPAUN-style architectures visually.

3. **brian-team/brian2**
   - Stars: ~900+
   - License: CeCILL (GPL-compatible)
   - URL: https://github.com/brian-team/brian2
   - Spiking neural network simulator. Written in Python, equations-oriented. Good for implementing STDP and Hebbian learning experiments.

4. **NEST/nest-simulator**
   - Stars: ~500+
   - License: GPL-2.0
   - URL: https://github.com/nest/nest-simulator
   - Large-scale spiking neural network simulator. Used by the Human Brain Project. Handles millions of neurons with biologically realistic dynamics.

### Active Inference and Free Energy Principle
5. **infer-actively/pymdp**

   - License: MIT
   - URL: https://github.com/infer-actively/pymdp

6. **SPFlow / active-inference-institute repos**

### Swarm Intelligence and Stigmergy

## 4. Books (behind paywalls unless noted — Owner to acquire)

1. **"Other Minds: The Octopus, the Sea, and the Deep Origins of Consciousness"**
   - Author: Peter Godfrey-Smith
   - Year: 2016 (Farrar, Straus and Giroux)
   - Why: THE book on cephalopod cognition and the evolution of consciousness. Godfrey-Smith is a philosopher of biology who actually dives with octopuses. Foundational for understanding distributed nervous systems and the evolutionary divergence of intelligence. Core to L1, L2, L7.

2. **"How to Build a Brain: A Neural Architecture for Biological Cognition"**
   - Author: Chris Eliasmith
   - Year: 2013 (Oxford University Press)
   - Why: Eliasmith's own book on the Neural Engineering Framework and SPAUN. The computational neuroscience bible for OpenBrainLM. Covers how to encode meaning in neural populations, transform representations, and build functional brain models.

3. **"The Free Energy Principle: A Unified Theory for the Brain?"**
   - Author: Karl Friston (various collected works)
   - Note: No single "Friston book" — use his 2010 Nature Reviews Neuroscience paper + "Active Inference" (2022, MIT Press, by Thomas Parr, Giovanni Pezzulo, and Karl J. Friston)
   - Why: Active inference book is the most accessible entry point to Friston's framework. Covers free energy minimization, prediction error, and how organisms maintain homeostasis through action and perception.

4. **"Honeybee Democracy"**
   - Author: Thomas Seeley
   - Year: 2010 (Princeton University Press)
   - Why: Seeley's magnum opus on how bee swarms make collective decisions. Documents quorum sensing, waggle dance communication, and democratic nest-site selection. Direct biological basis for L6 relevance detection.

5. **"The Superorganism: The Beauty, Elegance, and Strangeness of Insect Societies"**
   - Authors: Bert Holldobler, E.O. Wilson
   - Year: 2008 (W.W. Norton)
   - Why: Wilson (the father of sociobiology) and Holldobler on insect colony superorganisms. Covers stigmergy, division of labor, and emergent complexity from simple rules. Foundation for L3.

6. **"Principles of Neural Science"**
   - Authors: Eric Kandel, James Schwartz, Thomas Jessell, Steven Siegelbaum, A.J. Hudspeth
   - Year: 6th edition (2021, McGraw-Hill)
   - Why: THE neuroscience textbook. 1700+ pages covering every neural mechanism OpenBrainLM models — synaptic plasticity, basal ganglia, hippocampus, amygdala, cortical architecture. Reference, not cover-to-cover reading.

7. **"Consciousness: An Introduction"**
   - Author: Susan Blackmore (with Emily Troscianko in later editions)
   - Year: 3rd edition (2018, Routledge)
   - Why: Best survey textbook on consciousness theories — covers GWT, IIT, Higher Order Theories, predictive processing, and the hard problem. Good for getting multiple perspectives in one source.

8. **"Swarm Intelligence: From Natural to Artificial Systems"**
   - Authors: Eric Bonabeau, Marco Dorigo, Guy Theraulaz
   - Year: 1999 (Oxford University Press)
   - Why: The canonical academic text on swarm intelligence. Covers ant colony optimization, stigmergy, self-organization. Mathematical treatment of how colony-level computation emerges from local agent rules.

9. **"The Ancient Origins of Consciousness: How the Brain Created Experience"**
   - Author: Todd Feinberg, Jon Mallatt
   - Year: 2016 (MIT Press)
   - Why: Traces consciousness back to the Cambrian explosion. Relevant to OpenBrainLM's use of multiple evolutionary substrates (insect, octopus, human) as design layers.

10. **"Ants at Work: How an Insect Society is Organized"**
    - Author: Deborah Gordon
    - Year: 1999 (Free Press)
    - Why: Gordon's accessible treatment of ant colony organization without central control. She demonstrates that no ant "knows" the colony's state — behavior emerges from interaction rates.

## 7. Notes for Owner

1. **YouTube search instructions**: Search queries are provided rather than direct URLs because specific video URLs change frequently. The named speakers (Godfrey-Smith, Eliasmith, Friston, Tononi, Baars, Seeley, Gordon) all have widely-available talks — search by name + topic.

2. **arXiv vs journal papers**: Several foundational papers in this domain were published in journals (Science, Nature Reviews Neuroscience, Cell) rather than arXiv. These are the Eliasmith SPAUN paper (Science 2012), Friston FEP paper (Nature Reviews Neuroscience 2010), and Liscovitch-Brauer RNA editing paper (Cell 2017). They may require institutional access or Sci-Hub. The active inference tutorial (2201.03904) IS on arXiv and is freely available.

3. **Nengo is the key repo**: Of all GitHub repos listed, nengo is the most directly relevant. It is the codebase behind SPAUN. Understanding Nengo's architecture (ensembles, connections, probes) provides a concrete reference for how OpenBrainLM's layers could be computationally implemented.

4. **Friston is hard**: Karl Friston's writing is notoriously dense. Start with the Ryan Smith tutorial paper (2201.03904) and the "Active Inference" book (Parr, Pezzulo, Friston 2022), NOT Friston's original papers. The YouTube talks by Machine Learning Street Talk with Friston are also more accessible than the papers.

5. **The three substrates**: OpenBrainLM's architecture draws from three evolutionary substrates — insect (L3 stigmergy/swarm), octopus (L1 sensing, L2 ganglion, L7 chromatophore), and human (L4-L6, L8). The source list is organized to ensure each substrate has adequate coverage. No single substrate should dominate the knowledge base.

6. **Cross-pollination targets**: Per Owner's brain region overlap strategy, the strongest sources should also feed into:
   - Region #02 (Agents/Arcs): SPAUN architecture, cognitive architecture repos (nupic, opencog)
   - Region #05 (Evolutionary ML): RNA editing papers, swarm optimization algorithms
   - Region #08 (Blood-Brain Barrier): Immune system / prediction error papers from Friston

7. **Consciousness theories are design constraints, not implementation targets**: OpenBrainLM does not claim to be conscious. GWT and IIT are included because they provide architectural constraints — e.g., GWT's "global broadcast" maps to the orchestrator's ignition protocol, IIT's "integration" constrains how layers must communicate. They are engineering heuristics, not metaphysical commitments.

8. **Gordon vs Seeley**: Both study insect collective intelligence but from different angles. Gordon (ants) focuses on interaction rates and task allocation without central control. Seeley (bees) focuses on quorum-based decision-making. Both are needed — Gordon for L3 stigmergy, Seeley for L6 quorum sensing.
